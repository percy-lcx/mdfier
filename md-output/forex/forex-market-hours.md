# Forex Trading Hours: Best Times to Trade Forex

Forex trading hours run 24 hours a day, five days a week, from 22:00 UTC on Sunday (17:00 EST) to 22:00 UTC on Friday (17:00 EST), as trading rotates across 4 primary sessions: Sydney, Tokyo, London, and New York.

The best time to trade Forex is during the London-New York overlap (13:00 to 17:00 UTC), where liquidity peaks, spreads are tightest, and price movements are most consistent. The London session on its own is the second-best window, followed by the New York session and the Asian session.

Across the trading week, Wednesday and Thursday produce the highest and most consistent activity for Forex traders. Wednesday is anchored by FOMC decisions and ADP employment data when scheduled. Monday is the lowest-volume day, and Friday activity depends on whether NFP is scheduled.

## What are the Forex trading hours?

Forex trading hours refer to the specific times during the week when the [global foreign exchange market](what-is-forex-trading) is open for trading. The Forex market operates 24 hours a day, five days a week, from 22:00 UTC on Sunday to 22:00 UTC on Friday, as trading moves across major financial centres in different time zones.

There are 4 primary Forex trading sessions:

1. Sydney Forex session
2. Tokyo Forex session
3. London Forex session
4. New York Forex session

| Forex session | Session hours (UTC) | Session hours (EST) | Peak activity window (UTC) | Peak activity window (EST) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Sydney session | 22:00–07:00 | 17:00–02:00 | 22:00–00:00 | 17:00–19:00 |
| Tokyo session | 00:00–09:00 | 19:00–04:00 | 00:00–03:00 | 19:00–22:00 |
| London session | 08:00–17:00 | 03:00–12:00 | 08:00–11:00 | 03:00–06:00 |
| New York session | 13:00–22:00 | 08:00–17:00 | 13:00–17:00 | 08:00–12:00 |

### When is the Sydney Forex session?

The Sydney Forex session runs from 22:00 to 07:00 UTC (17:00 to 02:00 EST) and is the first session to open each week. Sydney is the primary interbank centre for AUD and NZD liquidity, and RBA and RBNZ rate decisions fall within this window when scheduled. This is the lowest-liquidity major session of the trading day, with wider spreads and range-bound price action outside of central bank announcements. The first hour of the Sunday open (22:00 to 23:00 UTC) is the thinnest stretch of the entire trading week, where gaps from weekend news are common.

### When is the Tokyo Forex session?

The Tokyo Forex session runs from 00:00 to 09:00 UTC (19:00 to 04:00 EST) and is centred on Japan's financial markets, the largest Forex trading centre in Asia. BOJ rate decisions, Japanese GDP, and Tankan surveys drive USD/JPY, EUR/JPY, and GBP/JPY during this window. Chinese economic data, including manufacturing PMI and trade balance figures, also falls within this session and affects AUD/USD and NZD/USD through risk sentiment. Liquidity is higher than Sydney but lower than London and New York, and the session overlaps briefly with both.

### When is the London Forex session?

The London Forex session runs from 08:00 to 17:00 UTC (03:00 to 12:00 EST) and is the largest Forex trading session by volume. London is the world's leading interbank Forex centre, and institutional participation is high from the open. Eurozone GDP, manufacturing PMI, and BOE rate decisions drive EUR and GBP pairs in the early session, while ECB interest rate decisions at 13:15 UTC (08:15 EST) are one of the highest-impact scheduled events for EUR/USD. Spreads on major pairs are tighter than any other single session and compress further when the New York overlap begins at 13:00 UTC (08:00 EST).

### When is the New York Forex session?

The New York Forex session runs from 13:00 to 22:00 UTC (08:00 to 17:00 EST) and is the second-largest interbank Forex centre globally. The first four hours overlap with London, creating the highest-liquidity period of the trading day. NFP, CPI, and retail sales releases at 13:30 UTC (08:30 EST) produce the sharpest short-term moves across USD pairs, and FOMC decisions at 19:00 UTC (14:00 EST) extend volatility into the late session. Once London closes at 17:00 UTC (12:00 EST), liquidity drops, spreads widen, and directional moves shorten in duration.

## When are the best times for trading Forex?

The best times for trading Forex are the hours when two major trading sessions overlap. These overlap windows increase market liquidity, tighten spreads, and create stronger price movement.

The best time to trade Forex falls within 4 time windows, ordered from highest to lowest activity:

1. London-New York overlap
2. London session
3. New York session (post-overlap)
4. Asian session (Sydney, Tokyo)

The ranking is based on liquidity in the Forex market. High liquidity produces tighter spreads, faster order execution, and more consistent price movements. These conditions reduce trading costs and lower the risk of slippage on entry and exit.

Liquidity in the Forex market is not evenly distributed across the trading day. It concentrates around the opening and closing of major financial centres, and peaks when two sessions run simultaneously.

Each window carries a different liquidity profile, spread behaviour, and set of price drivers.

| Session | Open (UTC) | Close (UTC) | Open (EST) | Close (EST) | Liquidity | Spread | Key price drivers |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| London–New York overlap | 13:00 | 17:00 | 08:00 | 12:00 | Highest | Tightest | NFP, CPI, FOMC releases (13:30 UTC), peak interbank volume in EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY |
| London session | 08:00 | 17:00 | 03:00 | 12:00 | High | Tight | European PMI and GDP releases (08:00–10:00 UTC), ECB decisions (13:15 UTC), London interbank pricing |
| New York session (post-overlap) | 17:00 | 22:00 | 12:00 | 17:00 | Moderate | Moderate | FOMC decisions (19:00 UTC), ISM figures, Treasury auctions, US afternoon positioning |
| Asian session (Sydney / Tokyo) | 22:00 | 08:00 | 17:00 | 03:00 | Lowest | Widest | RBA/RBNZ rate decisions (variable), Chinese PMI (variable), position management |

The liquidity ranking above is driven by 3 session overlaps during the trading day. Each overlap concentrates activity in different currency pairs:

| --- | --- | --- | --- |

| London / New York | 13:00 – 17:00 | 08:00 – 12:00 | EUR/USD,GBP/USD,USD/JPY,USD/CHF |

### Sydney-Tokyo Forex session overlap

The Sydney–Tokyo overlap occurs when both the Sydney session and the Tokyo session are open simultaneously, from 00:00 to 02:00 UTC (19:00 to 21:00 EST).

The most actively traded currency pairs during this overlap include AUD/JPY, AUD/USD, NZD/USD and USD/JPY. Trading activity is concentrated in AUD and JPY pairs as Japanese institutions enter the market while Australian markets remain open.

### Tokyo-London Forex session overlap

The Tokyo–London overlap occurs when both the Tokyo session and the London session are open simultaneously, from 08:00 to 09:00 UTC (03:00 to 04:00 EST).

The most actively traded currency pairs during this overlap include EUR/JPY, GBP/JPY, USD/JPY and EUR/USD. Trading activity increases in JPY crosses as European traders enter the market and respond to price levels established during the Asian session.

### London-New York Forex session overlap

The London–New York overlap occurs when both the London session and the New York session are open simultaneously, from 13:00 to 17:00 UTC (08:00 to 12:00 EST).

The most actively traded currency pairs during this overlap include EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY and USD/CHF. Trading activity is highest during this window because European and US institutions participate at the same time, which increases liquidity and tightens spreads in major currency pairs.

## What economic events affect Forex trading?

Five categories of economic events move Forex prices significantly, regardless of which session they fall in:

1. US Federal Reserve (FOMC) decisions
2. US Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP)
3. US inflation data (CPI)
4. European Central Bank (ECB) interest rate decisions
5. Geopolitical events

| Event | Release frequency | Release time (UTC) | Release time (EST) | Impact on Forex |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| FOMC interest rate decision | 8 times per year | 19:00 | 14:00 | Highest |
| FOMC press conference | 8 times per year | 19:30 | 14:30 | Highest |
| US Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) | First Friday of each month | 13:30 | 08:30 | High |
| US Consumer Price Index (CPI) | Monthly | 13:30 | 08:30 | High |
| ECB interest rate decision | 8 times per year | 13:15 | 08:15 | High |
| Geopolitical events | Unscheduled | Variable | Variable | High |

**1. US Federal Reserve (FOMC) decisions**

FOMC interest rate decisions are the single largest scheduled catalyst for Forex markets. The Federal Reserve sets the US federal funds rate, which directly influences the value of the USD against all other currencies.

- Rate hikes strengthen the USD and pressure non-dollar pairs lower.
- Rate cuts weaken the USD and support non-dollar pairs.

Decisions are released at 19:00 UTC (14:00 EST), with press conferences following at 19:30 UTC (14:30 EST). Forward guidance in the press conference can extend volatility across USD pairs for the remainder of the New York session.

**2. US Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP)**

NFP is released on the first Friday of each month at 13:30 UTC (08:30 EST). It measures the net change in US employment and is the most closely watched labour market indicator in Forex.

- Strong employment figures strengthen the USD by raising expectations for tighter Federal Reserve policy.
- Weak figures weaken the USD by signalling economic slowdown.

NFP deviations from consensus forecasts produce the sharpest short-term moves of the month across EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY, and USD/CHF.

**3. US inflation data (CPI)**

The US Consumer Price Index is released monthly at 13:30 UTC (08:30 EST). CPI measures the rate of price change across a basket of consumer goods and services.

- Rising inflation increases the probability of Federal Reserve rate hikes, which strengthens the USD.
- Falling inflation reduces that probability and weakens the USD.

CPI releases consistently produce sharp directional moves in all major USD pairs within minutes of publication.

**4. European Central Bank (ECB) interest rate decisions**

ECB interest rate decisions are released 8 times per year at 13:15 UTC (08:15 EST), with press conferences following at 13:45 UTC (08:45 EST). The ECB sets monetary policy for the eurozone, and its rate decisions directly affect EUR/USD, EUR/GBP, and all EUR crosses. Rate divergence between the ECB and Federal Reserve is one of the primary drivers of EUR/USD direction over multi-week periods.

**5. Geopolitical events**

Wars, sanctions, trade disputes, and financial crises drive demand for safe-haven currencies such as USD, JPY, and CHF, and weaken risk-sensitive currencies such as AUD, NZD, and emerging market pairs. These events are unscheduled and can produce significant price moves during any session, including the Asian session where liquidity is low and resistance to sharp moves is minimal.

All 5 categories share one common effect: they concentrate volume and volatility around a specific timestamp or event, temporarily overriding the session-based liquidity patterns described above. Forex traders should check the economic calendar before opening positions, particularly during lower-liquidity sessions where a data release or headline can move prices sharply with less resistance.

## Which days of the week are best for trading Forex?

Wednesday and Thursday are the best days of the week to trade Forex. The breakdown below covers what drives activity on each trading day.

| Day | Activity level | Key events | Key event time (UTC) | Key event time (EST) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Monday | Low | No major scheduled releases, geopolitical reassessment | N/A | N/A |
| Tuesday | Moderate | European PMI releases, US consumer confidence | 08:00–10:00 / 15:00 | 03:00–05:00 / 10:00 |
| Wednesday | Highest | FOMC decisions, ADP employment data, EIA report | 19:00 / 13:15 / 15:30 | 14:00 / 08:15 / 10:30 |
| Thursday | High | US jobless claims, ECB interest rate decisions | 13:30 | 08:30 |
| Friday | Variable | NFP (first Friday), weekend position reduction | 13:30 | 08:30 |

### Forex trading on Monday

Monday is the lowest-volume day of the trading week. Institutional traders re-enter the market after the weekend and spend the early session assessing geopolitical and macroeconomic developments that occurred while markets were closed. Price movements are present but lack the sustained directional momentum seen mid-week. Spreads can be slightly wider in the early Monday session before full liquidity returns, particularly during the Sydney and early Tokyo hours.

### Forex trading on Tuesday

Tuesday is the first day of the week where full institutional participation resumes across all four sessions. Volume increases relative to Monday and price movements become more consistent. European PMI releases between 08:00 and 10:00 UTC (03:00 and 05:00 EST) can move EUR and GBP pairs. US consumer confidence data, released at 15:00 UTC (10:00 EST), affects USD sentiment and feeds through to all major pairs.

### Forex trading on Wednesday

Wednesday is the most active day of the trading week for Forex. FOMC interest rate decisions and meeting minutes are released on Wednesdays when scheduled, making this the highest-impact day for USD pairs. US ADP employment data, released at 13:15 UTC (08:15 EST) on the first Wednesday of each month, moves major pairs ahead of the Friday NFP release. The EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report at 15:30 UTC (10:30 EST) can also affect commodity-linked currencies such as CAD through its impact on oil prices.

### Forex trading on Thursday

Thursday maintains high activity levels. US jobless claims data is released weekly at 13:30 UTC (08:30 EST) and produces consistent short-term moves across USD pairs by signalling the health of the US labour market. European Central Bank interest rate decisions are scheduled on Thursdays when applicable, directly affecting EUR/USD and EUR crosses. Positioning from Wednesday's FOMC-related moves often extends into Thursday as traders adjust.

### Forex trading on Friday

Friday activity depends on the scheduled calendar. Non-Farm Payrolls are released at 13:30 UTC (08:30 EST) on the first Friday of each month, producing the single largest scheduled weekly move across all major USD pairs. NFP figures that deviate from consensus forecasts can shift USD sentiment for the remainder of the session.

Outside of NFP Fridays, activity tapers in the latter half of the New York session as traders reduce exposure ahead of the weekend market close at 22:00 UTC (17:00 EST). Liquidity thins noticeably after 20:00 UTC (15:00 EST), and spreads widen as the weekly close approaches.

## Best Forex Trading Sessions FAQs

### Does Forex trade 24/7?

No, Forex does not trade 24/7. The Forex market operates 24 hours a day, five days a week, but it closes for the weekend.

Trading runs continuously from 22:00 UTC on Sunday (17:00 EST) to 22:00 UTC on Friday (17:00 EST). During this period, market activity moves across the Sydney, Tokyo, London, and New York sessions without interruption.

Outside of these hours, the interbank market shuts down and most Forex instruments become unavailable. Some brokers provide limited weekend trading on selected products, but pricing is typically internal and liquidity is lower than during the standard trading week.

### Does Forex trade on weekends?

No, there is no Forex trading on weekends as the global interbank market is closed.

Weekend confusion usually comes from time zone differences. The market may reopen early Monday morning local time depending on where you live, even though it officially opens on Sunday at 22:00 UTC. Likewise, trading may appear to end on Saturday morning in certain regions due to time conversion.

Most currency pairs are paused over the weekend. However, some brokers offer limited weekend access to selected instruments. In addition, cryptocurrency pairs such as BTC/USD remain tradable on Saturday and Sunday because crypto markets operate continuously.

Forex trading resumes once the global market reopens at the start of the new trading week.

### When is the best time to trade EUR/USD?

The best time to trade EUR/USD is during the London–New York session overlap, typically between 13:00 and 17:00 UTC (08:00 to 12:00 EST). EUR/USD accounts for roughly one-fifth of total global FX turnover, and this window aligns peak European banking activity with the US market open, concentrating the highest executable volume and tightening spreads as institutional capital flows increase on both sides of the pair.

### When is the best time to trade GBP/USD?

The best time to trade GBP/USD is during the London–New York session overlap, typically between 13:00 and 17:00 UTC (08:00 to 12:00 EST). This period aligns the core UK trading day with US institutional participation, increasing sterling and dollar transaction flow and producing stronger intraday range expansion compared to single-session hours.

### When is the best time to trade USD/JPY?

The best time to trade USD/JPY is during the London–New York session overlap, typically between 13:00 and 17:00 UTC (08:00 to 12:00 EST). The US dollar is involved in nearly 90% of global FX transactions, and when US institutions enter the market, dollar liquidity intensifies against the yen even after the Tokyo session closes, increasing directional volatility.

### When is the best time to trade NZD/USD?

The best time to trade NZD/USD is during the Sydney–Tokyo overlap, typically between 00:00 and 02:00 UTC (19:00 to 21:00 EST). This window concentrates Asia-Pacific institutional participation, increasing transaction depth in smaller commodity-linked currencies such as NZD and improving liquidity compared to later single-session hours.

### When is the best time to trade AUD/JPY?

The best time to trade AUD/JPY is during the Sydney–Tokyo overlap, typically between 00:00 and 02:00 UTC (19:00 to 21:00 EST). This overlap brings together Australian commodity-linked flows and Japanese institutional liquidity, and AUD/JPY often records its strongest hourly ranges when both markets are active simultaneously.

### When is the best time to trade USD/CHF?

The best time to trade USD/CHF is during the London–New York session overlap, typically between 13:00 and 17:00 UTC (08:00 to 12:00 EST). This period increases turnover as European capital flows interact with US dollar demand, strengthening liquidity in the Swiss franc during peak transatlantic trading hours.

### When is the best time to trade GBP/JPY?

The best time to trade GBP/JPY is during the Tokyo–London overlap, typically between 08:00 and 09:00 UTC (03:00 to 04:00 EST). This transition hour combines residual yen liquidity from Asia with fresh sterling positioning from London, and GBP/JPY frequently experiences above-average hourly volatility during this session handover.
