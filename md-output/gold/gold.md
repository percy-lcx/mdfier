You expect gold prices to rise and open a **long Gold CFD** position of **0.1 lot (10 ounces) at USD $2,000 per ounce**, giving a total position value of **USD $20,000**. This represents a **50% return on the USD $1,000** margin committed. If the price instead falls to USD $1,970 per ounce, closing the trade results in a loss of USD $300, or a 30% loss on your margin.

## Gold CFD FAQs

### What are gold CFDs?

Gold CFDs (Contracts for Difference) allow traders to speculate on gold price movements without owning physical gold. A gold CFD is a financial derivative contract offered by a broker, created as an agreement between the trader and the broker, and is based on the gold price rather than the underlying asset. Gold CFDs are price derivatives that mirror movements in spot gold prices, which are closely aligned with gold futures pricing, enabling traders to [participate in the gold market](what-is-gold-trading) by trading changes in the gold price itself rather than buying or selling physical gold.

### How does gold CFD trading work?

Gold CFD trading works by opening a trade based on whether you expect the gold price to rise or fall and exchanging the difference in price when the position is closed. Gold CFDs are traded on margin, meaning only a portion of the full trade value is required upfront, while profits and losses are calculated on the full position size. The main costs involved in Gold CFD trading typically include the spread, any applicable commissions, and overnight financing charges for positions held open beyond a trading day.

There are 5 benefits of trading Gold CFDs:

- Lower capital requirements through leverageGold CFD trading lets traders gain larger market exposure with a lower upfront capital rquirement, as CFDs are traded on margin rather than requiring full contract value upfront.
- High liquidity and efficient pricingGold CFDs are highly liquid derivatives, which helps support fast execution and competitive spreads in active market conditions.
- Lower trading costsGold CFDs typically have lower transaction costs than many alternative instruments, making them a more cost-effective way to trade gold price movements.
- Useful for diversification and hedgingGold CFDs are suitable for diversification or hedging against broader market risk due to gold's relatively lower volatility and strong liquidity profile.

### What is an example of trading gold CFD?

An example of trading a Gold CFD is when a trader expects gold prices to rise and opens a long position of 0.1 lot (10 ounces) at USD $2,000 per ounce with 1:20 leverage, requiring only USD $1,000 in margin to control a USD $20,000 position.

If the gold price later rises to USD $2,050 per ounce, the trader closes the position and makes a gross profit of USD $500 (USD 50 × 10 ounces), excluding spreads and trading costs.

If the gold price instead falls to USD $1,970 per ounce, the trader closes the trade at a loss of USD $300 (USD 30 × 10 ounces), excluding spreads and trading costs.

### What are the costs of trading gold CFDs?

The cost of trading gold CFDs include the spread, any commission the broker charges, and overnight financing if you hold positions after the trading day. These charges affect the full exposure of the position, so understanding them before trading is important.

### What are the gold CFD trading hours?

Gold CFD trading hours run almost 24 hours a day, five days a week, based on UTC. Trading typically opens at 22:00 UTC on Sunday and closes at 21:59 UTC on Friday, covering the Asian, European, and US trading sessions.

A short daily trading break usually occurs around 21:59–22:00 UTC for rollover and settlement, depending on the broker, after which trading resumes immediately.
